about

I am a data journalist and builder of web things at the Hindustan Times in New Delhi, India. My work here ranges from analysing how politicians spend their time in Parliament to making award-winning graphics about Bollywood music.


Previously, I was a part of the investigative team at the Milwaukee Journal Sentinel where I created data visualizations and news apps for enterprise projects.


Before that, I was at the The Palm Beach Post where I got to make data queries for health stories, make news apps about politics and scrape public data. Sometimes, I also got to make baseball cards with the faces of council members.


I was also a Google Journalism Fellow at Poynter where I reported on the media industry, highlighting innovation and technology in journalism.


I am a 2015 graduate of Columbia Journalism School, where I graduated with honors.
