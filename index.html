about

I am a student at the Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism. A strong believer of the power of technology and a big fan of the term ‘new media,’ I believe in telling multimedia stories that are not limited to the 800-word format. After all, the digital space is an interactive one, and it is best when kept that way.


Most recently, I have started doing cool things in R, and even cooler things in JavaScript.


When not exploring the world of data and interactive storytelling, I write about technology and social media at different online portals.


When there are brain waves that don’t fit in ‘different portals,’ they end up in my blog.

City Bikes? Brooklyn Wants More

The Brooklyn Ink and Columbia Journalist

words + code + data

Before Citi Bike launched in 2013, the Department of Transportation (DOT) released a suggestion portal, where people could suggest future Citi Bike station locations. I scraped the data collected by the online portal and analyzed it to understand where these requests were coming from and whether the demand followed up with supply. Tools: R, Python

Here Is What’s Gone Wrong With Delhi University’s 4-year Undergraduate Programme

Business Insider India

words

In 2013, Delhi University - the largest higher education institution in India shifted from a three-year undergraduate program to a four-year program. One semester after the change, in a survey conducted by the teachers' association, academic committees of 44 colleges announced that they were against the program. Through extensive interviews with teachers and students both in favour and against the program, this piece listed down the problems underlying the shift.
